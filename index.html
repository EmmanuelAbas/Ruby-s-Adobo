Red hot Pork Sisig Recipe and procedures to cook by Chef Ruby!

First of all what is Sisig and it's History?

Sisig, a well-loved Filipino dish, originated in Pampanga and has a rich history dating back to the 1700s. The term "sisig" is derived from the Kapampangan word meaning "to snack on something sour," as the original versions of the dish involved fruits or vegetables marinated in vinegar and salt. Over time, it evolved into a savory dish using pig's head and ears boiled, grilled, and mixed with vinegar, calamansi, onions, and spices. In the 1970s, Lucia "Aling Lucing" Cunanan from Angeles City transformed sisig into the sizzling version we know today by grilling the ingredients and serving them hot. This innovation helped popularize sisig across the Philippines and globally, with variations including chicken, seafood, and tofu. The dish has become a cultural symbol and is celebrated annually in Pampanga's Sisig Festival.

Ingredients you need for Ruby's Sisig recipe:
  • 1 kilogram pork liempo
  • 1 whole garlic
  • 2 whole onion(preferably medium size)
  • 1 table spoon of whole peppercorn
  • 6 long green chili
  • 3 red chili peppers(optional)
  • 1 teaspoon of ground black pepper(pamintang durog)
  • 3 table spoon of mayonnaise
  • 1 liter of cooking oil

Ruby^

Step by step procedure for cooking
  1. First get a medium sized cooking pot and put the pork liempo and add water(until fully submerged), 1 table spoon of whole Pepper corn and 1 whole garlic.Boil for 45 minutes or until tender.
  2. After boiling the pork liempo, rest it for 10 minutes and get a deep fryer then put the oil and fry the pork liempo until crispy.
  3. Now after frying the pork liempo, chop it in a small diced shaped together with the long green chilis, red chili pepper and onions.
  4. The next is to get a wok and saute the chopped long green chilis, red chili peppers and onion until fragrant in medium heat.
  5. Now add the chopped pork liempo to the wok and mix for about 30 seconds.
  6. After 30 seconds add the mayonnaise and ground black pepper and mix for about 1 minute in medium heat
  7. Lastly put it in a plate or even a sizzling plate to enjoy!
